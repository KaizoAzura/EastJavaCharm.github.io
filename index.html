Status

Description

Mount Bromo is an active volcano located in East Java, Indonesia. It is part of the Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park, which is one of the most visited tourist attractions in Java. The volcano stands at approximately 2,329 meters (7,641 feet) tall and is renowned for its stunning sunrise views and otherworldly landscapes.

The history of Mount Bromo is deeply intertwined with the geological and cultural heritage of the region. Its formation is believed to date back thousands of years, with eruptions shaping its distinctive crater and surrounding landscapes over time. The volcano has a history of periodic eruptions, with notable events recorded throughout the centuries.

Ijen crater

Height

2.769 m

Location

East Java, Banyuwangi

Status

Active

Geograph

8.058°S 114.242°E

Description

Kawah Ijen is renowned for its stunning turquoise-colored acidic crater lake, which is one of the largest in the world. The lake is approximately 1 kilometer in diameter and contains highly acidic water with a pH as low as 0.5. The unique color of the lake is due to the presence of dissolved sulfur and other minerals.

One of the most distinctive features of Kawah Ijen is the presence of sulfur vents and fumaroles around the crater rim. These vents emit volcanic gases, including sulfur dioxide, which ignite upon contact with the air, creating mesmerizing blue flames known as "blue fire." The blue flames are most visible at night, offering a spectacular natural phenomenon.

Despite its popularity as a tourist destination, Kawah Ijen faces environmental challenges due to sulfur mining activities and the impact of tourism. Efforts are underway to promote sustainable tourism practices and conserve the fragile ecosystem of the crater and its surrounding areas.

Description

Papuma Beach is a stunning coastal destination located in Jember, East Java, Indonesia. Known for its pristine white sandy beaches and crystal-clear turquoise waters, Papuma Beach offers visitors a tranquil escape surrounded by natural beauty.

The beach is ideal for swimming, snorkeling, and surfing, with gentle waves and a refreshing sea breeze. Visitors can also explore the nearby cliffs and caves or simply relax and enjoy the picturesque sunset views.

Papuma Beach is equipped with various facilities, including beach huts, restaurants serving fresh seafood, showers, and ample parking space. It is accessible by car or motorcycle from the city center of Jember, making it a popular weekend getaway for locals and tourists alike.

About us

Welcome to East Java, a land of diverse cultures, natural wonders, and rich heritage. Situated in the eastern part of the Indonesian archipelago, East Java boasts a unique blend of tradition and modernity, offering visitors a captivating journey through history, nature, and hospitality.

East Java is home to a melting pot of cultures, with influences from Javanese, Madurese, Chinese, Arabic, and other ethnic groups. This diversity is reflected in the region's language, art, music, dance, and cuisine, creating a vibrant tapestry of traditions that captivates the senses and inspires exploration.

From majestic volcanoes to pristine beaches and lush rainforests, East Java is blessed with a wealth of natural beauty. Mount Bromo, with its ethereal landscapes and breathtaking sunrises, is an iconic symbol of the region's volcanic legacy. Meanwhile, destinations like Papuma Beach and the Ijen Crater offer opportunities for relaxation and adventure amidst stunning coastal scenery and volcanic landscapes.

East Java's rich history is evident in its ancient temples, colonial architecture, and archaeological sites. The majestic Borobudur and Prambanan temples stand as testament to the region's glorious past, while the remnants of Dutch colonial buildings in cities like Surabaya and Malang offer glimpses into Indonesia's colonial heritage.

One of the highlights of visiting East Java is experiencing the warmth and hospitality of its people. Known for their friendliness and generosity, the locals welcome visitors with open arms, inviting them to immerse themselves in the region's cultural traditions and culinary delights. From savory dishes like rawon and soto ayam to sweet treats like onde-onde and lapis legit, East Java's cuisine tantalizes the taste buds and leaves a lasting impression.

As guardians of this natural and cultural treasure trove, East Java is committed to promoting sustainable tourism practices that preserve the region's beauty and heritage for future generations. Through community-based initiatives, conservation efforts, and responsible tourism development, East Java strives to create a balance between tourism and environmental preservation.

Location

The landscape of East Java is characterized by a diverse range of geographical features, including towering volcanoes, fertile plains, rolling hills, and picturesque coastlines. The region is dominated by a series of volcanic peaks, with Mount Semeru, the highest volcano on Java, standing at an impressive 3,676 meters above sea level.

East Java is easily accessible by air, land, and sea. The region is served by several airports, including Juanda International Airport in Surabaya and Abdul Rachman Saleh Airport in Malang, offering domestic and international flights to various destinations. Additionally, East Java is well-connected by a network of highways and railways, providing convenient transportation options for travelers exploring the region.
